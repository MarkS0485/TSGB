# Have I Got News For You
## "The Full Miliband" - A Special Edition
### Ed Miliband, Secretary of State for Energy Security and Net Zero, in the Chair

*Running time: approx. 30 minutes*
*Recorded before a live studio audience*
*Produced by Hat Trick Productions for BBC One*

---

> *A NOTE ON FORMAT:*
> *This is written in full HIGNFY transcript style - the host opens with autocue monologue, rounds follow, Hislop and Merton attack from both sides throughout, and the host closes with the traditional sign-off. Stage directions are in italics. The Deayton scandal is the ghost in the room: Merton was the man who stabbed Deayton in the front, and both he and Hislop know exactly how to use a sitting target. Miliband is the target.*

---

## OPENING TITLES

*Theme music. Titles roll.*

**ANNOUNCER (V.O.):** Tonight on Have I Got News For You - Ian Hislop, editor of Private Eye and a man who has never let the facts get in the way of a good lawsuit. Paul Merton, comedian, and the only man in Britain who has driven two television presenters off the air without technically saying anything actionable. And in the chair - the Secretary of State for Energy Security and Net Zero, the man responsible for the British grid, and, as of last Tuesday, the British dark - Ed Miliband.

*Miliband enters to enormous applause, sits down, shuffles papers, looks at autocue, pulls a face.*

---

## OPENING MONOLOGUE

**MILIBAND:** *(reading carefully)* Good evening and welcome to Have I Got News For You, where tonight's host is the man who promised to cut your energy bills by three hundred pounds, and whose department's own impact assessment says he can't, won't, and hasn't - but who has, to be fair, kept that document hidden, so technically you don't know that yet.

*Audience laughs. Miliband looks slightly alarmed at what he just read.*

**MILIBAND:** I should say - I didn't write that. *(looks at autocue again)* Though apparently I can't say I didn't commission it.

**MERTON:** *(to Hislop)* He's going to be wonderful.

**HISLOP:** *(to Merton)* Give it thirty seconds.

**MILIBAND:** *(soldiering on)* On Ian Hislop's team tonight -

**MERTON:** He's doing a Boris! He's reading the team names!

*Laughter.*

**MILIBAND:** *(stops, mortified)* I wasn't -

**HISLOP:** You absolutely were.

**MILIBAND:** I was checking the format -

**MERTON:** The format is: *don't read Ian's team out loud.*

**MILIBAND:** *(composing himself, ignores autocue entirely)* Right. We'll do this my way. The energy system of Great Britain is undergoing a historic and transformative -

**HISLOP:** Can we do the show first?

**MILIBAND:** That *was* the show.

**HISLOP:** Was it?

**MILIBAND:** It was the *vision.*

**MERTON:** Angus Deayton had a vision. Didn't end well.

*Pause. Miliband blinks.*

**MILIBAND:** I'm not sure that's an appropriate -

**HISLOP:** He brought it up.

**MERTON:** I bring a lot of things up. It's a gift.

---

## ROUND ONE - THE ODD ONE OUT

**MILIBAND:** Right. Round one. Ian, your picture round. Three items - which is the odd one out?

*Screen shows: a spinning top / a gyroscope / a NESO press release*

**HISLOP:** *(immediately)* The NESO press release.

**MILIBAND:** And why is that?

**HISLOP:** Because the other two have *actual inertia.*

*Laughter.*

**MILIBAND:** That's - that's a very specific answer.

**HISLOP:** I read the technical documents, Ed. I'm an editor.

**MILIBAND:** It's a *transition.*

**HISLOP:** From spinning to not spinning, yes. We've noticed.

**MERTON:** I thought inertia was when something keeps doing what it's already doing.

**HISLOP:** Exactly. The grid stopped spinning. The policy kept going. That's the inertia.

**MILIBAND:** *(gamely)* The grid has *synthetic* inertia now -

**MERTON:** What's synthetic inertia?

**MILIBAND:** It's - it's when a battery *responds very quickly* to simulate the effect of -

**MERTON:** How quickly?

**MILIBAND:** Two hundred and fifty milliseconds.

**MERTON:** And the real thing?

**MILIBAND:** *(beat)* ...Instantly.

**MERTON:** So you replaced *instant* with *two hundred and fifty milliseconds.*

**MILIBAND:** In a controlled and certified -

**HISLOP:** And the grid failed in eighty milliseconds.

**MILIBAND:** That's a contested figure -

**HISLOP:** Ed. The lights went out. Before the batteries woke up.

**MILIBAND:** The *response was within specification.*

**MERTON:** Of what?

**MILIBAND:** Of the specification.

**MERTON:** Which you wrote.

**MILIBAND:** Which NESO -

**HISLOP:** Under your direction.

**MILIBAND:** Under a *process.*

**MERTON:** Processes. That's what we need. More processes. That'll sort the physics out.

*Audience applause.*

---

## ROUND TWO - MISSING WORDS

**MILIBAND:** Round two - Missing Words. Paul, this is from a recent NESO consultation document. Fill in the blank: "The minimum system inertia requirement can be reduced while maintaining system security through enhanced ___________."

**MERTON:** *(long pause)* Prayers?

*Laughter.*

**MERTON:** Dynamic Containment?

**MILIBAND:** That is actually correct -

**HISLOP:** The answer is "firmware pretending to be a flywheel."

**MILIBAND:** That's not - that's not the official -

**HISLOP:** It's the *accurate* answer. There is a difference, as your department has discovered.

**MILIBAND:** Dynamic Containment is a highly sophisticated -

**MERTON:** Sophisticated. Right. Sophisticated enough to arrive after the grid had collapsed?

**MILIBAND:** The grid did *not* collapse -

**HISLOP:** Ed, eleven hours.

**MILIBAND:** There were *extenuating circumstances.*

**MERTON:** A wind farm turned off.

**MILIBAND:** One wind farm, in a low-inertia window, during a -

**MERTON:** How many wind farms does it take to turn the lights off?

**MILIBAND:** *(carefully)* ...One, in the right conditions.

**MERTON:** And what conditions were those?

**MILIBAND:** Conditions that we are now reviewing.

**HISLOP:** "Conditions we are reviewing" - that's the department that created them reviewing them?

**MILIBAND:** We have an *independent* panel -

**HISLOP:** Appointed by you.

**MILIBAND:** Appointed through a *process.*

**MERTON:** There's that word again.

*Hislop produces a copy of Private Eye. The headline reads: "MILIBAND: WE HAVE A PROCESS"*

**HISLOP:** We went to press early.

---

## INTERLUDE - THE AUTOCUE INCIDENT

*A producer is heard on talkback, faintly, saying something.*

**MILIBAND:** *(looking at earpiece)* I'm sorry, what? *(to camera, apologetically)* Apparently I need to - they want me to read this. *(looks at autocue, long pause)* I'm not reading that.

**MERTON:** What does it say?

**MILIBAND:** It says - *(laughing despite himself)* it says "Ed Miliband: from the department that delivers. Clean energy, occasionally. Electricity, subject to inertia constraints."

*Audience laughter.*

**MILIBAND:** I'm not reading that.

**HISLOP:** Boris always closed with a party-political message.

**MILIBAND:** I'm aware of Boris.

**MERTON:** We're all aware of Boris.

**HISLOP:** He promised coconuts once, you know.

**MERTON:** Did he deliver?

**HISLOP:** A crew member got them just in time.

**MERTON:** That's more than Ed's delivered on bills.

**MILIBAND:** We have delivered on - the Great British Energy -

**HISLOP:** Has one employee.

**MILIBAND:** It's *scaling up.*

**MERTON:** Like the synthetic inertia.

**MILIBAND:** They're not the same -

**MERTON:** No, the inertia is supposed to be faster.

*Audience applause.*

---

## ROUND THREE - CURRENT EVENTS

**MILIBAND:** Right. Current events. Paul - in the news this week: Ofgem has described the new energy framework as "smart, flexible, and future-ready." What happened next?

**MERTON:** *(immediately)* The lights went out?

*Laughter.*

**MILIBAND:** Specifically -

**MERTON:** No, no, I'm going with "the lights went out." That's my answer.

**MILIBAND:** *(checking card)* That's... technically correct, yes.

**MERTON:** I've been watching the news. It's very educational. When there's power.

**HISLOP:** *(to Miliband)* Ed, can I ask - did anyone at DESNZ ever actually read the physics?

**MILIBAND:** We have extensive technical expertise -

**HISLOP:** In your department.

**MILIBAND:** In our department and in NESO -

**HISLOP:** Then how does a 368-page net zero strategy mention the word "inertia" once?

**MILIBAND:** It's a *strategy* document. Not a textbook.

**HISLOP:** It's a strategy for a system that runs on physics.

**MILIBAND:** Which is *exactly* why we have engineers -

**HISLOP:** Below the policy layer.

**MILIBAND:** Working *within* the policy framework -

**HISLOP:** Doing what policy tells them regardless of what physics says.

**MILIBAND:** Physics and policy are not in conflict -

**MERTON:** They were last Tuesday.

*Laughter.*

**MILIBAND:** That was a *specific incident* in an -

**MERTON:** An unscheduled aquatic diversion?

**MILIBAND:** *That's* not - that's a different -

**HISLOP:** *(to Merton)* He's been briefed.

**MERTON:** *(to Miliband)* Who told you that?

**MILIBAND:** I'm not going to - that's not - we don't use that phrase internally.

**HISLOP:** What phrase do you use internally?

**MILIBAND:** *(carefully)* A *demand-side reconnection event.*

**MERTON:** That's "the lights went out" in four syllables more.

**MILIBAND:** *(quietly, to himself)* Seven.

**HISLOP:** Ed, did you count?

**MILIBAND:** I was - I'm very good with numbers.

**MERTON:** Are you? What's 260 minus 98?

**MILIBAND:** *(long pause)* ...162.

**MERTON:** That's how much system inertia you lost. In gigavolt-ampere-seconds.

**MILIBAND:** The inertia -

**MERTON:** 162.

**HISLOP:** Gone.

**MERTON:** Not replaced.

**HISLOP:** Replaced with a quarterly procurement window.

**MERTON:** And some very optimistic firmware.

**MILIBAND:** The Dynamic Containment programme is world-leading -

**HISLOP:** At arriving after things have gone wrong.

**MILIBAND:** At *responding to events - *

**HISLOP:** Which it cannot prevent.

**MILIBAND:** It *mitigates - *

**MERTON:** After.

**MILIBAND:** ...Yes. After.

**MERTON:** And the grid failed before.

**MILIBAND:** *(quietly)* ...Yes.

*Pause. The audience is very still for a moment.*

**MERTON:** *(gently)* Ed. You do know this, don't you.

**MILIBAND:** *(slightly broken)* We are conducting a very thorough review.

**HISLOP:** Is the review being conducted by the same people who approved the framework?

**MILIBAND:** By *independent experts - *

**HISLOP:** Appointed through the process.

**MILIBAND:** ...Yes.

**MERTON:** Will it recommend spinning steel?

**MILIBAND:** It will examine all options -

**HISLOP:** Including the one that worked for seventy years.

**MILIBAND:** The grid cannot run on fossil fuel inertia -

**MERTON:** It doesn't have to be fossil fuel. You can spin steel with a motor.

**MILIBAND:** *(pause)* ...Yes.

**MERTON:** Which is what a synchronous condenser is.

**MILIBAND:** *(another pause)* ...Yes.

**MERTON:** Which you could have kept when you retired the power stations.

*Very long pause.*

**MILIBAND:** We are investigating that option.

**MERTON:** Good.

**HISLOP:** It's in the documents, Ed. It's been in the documents for years.

**MILIBAND:** We are in the process of -

**MERTON:** *(softly)* Don't say process.

**MILIBAND:** *(softly)* ...reviewing the options.

**HISLOP:** *(not unkindly)* Ed. Is any of this in your impact assessment?

**MILIBAND:** *(beat)* ...Which one?

**HISLOP:** The one you haven't released.

**MILIBAND:** The one that - the document that was never - that is incomplete and would cause confusion -

**MERTON:** The one that says bills will go up.

**MILIBAND:** The one that - the *partial analysis - *

**HISLOP:** The one that said your policy was going to cost people money, which you suppressed, while simultaneously promising to save people three hundred pounds.

**MILIBAND:** *(very quietly)* ...We are committed to cutting bills by three hundred pounds by 2030.

**MERTON:** From what baseline?

**MILIBAND:** *(long pause)* ...Current levels.

**MERTON:** And current levels were set after the blackout?

**MILIBAND:** *(even longer pause)* ...Yes.

*Audience laughter, slightly pained.*

**HISLOP:** So bills went up because the grid failed -

**MERTON:** - because you removed the inertia -

**HISLOP:** - and you're promising to cut them from the new higher level -

**MERTON:** - by 2030 -

**HISLOP:** - which is five years away -

**MERTON:** - and the document showing whether that's possible -

**HISLOP:** - hasn't been published -

**MERTON:** - because it would cause "confusion."

**MILIBAND:** And misunderstanding.

*Beat.*

**MERTON:** *(to Hislop)* I think we've found someone who can replace Angus.

**HISLOP:** I think we've found someone *worse.*

*Miliband looks at the autocue. He stares at it for a long time. He seems to be making a decision.*

---

## SCORES AND CLOSING

**MILIBAND:** Right. Scores. Ian's team - twelve. Paul's team - eleven. And the Secretary of State for Energy Security and Net Zero - nil. *(beat)* Which is the same as the number of impact assessments I've published this year. I'll see myself out.

*Laughter and applause.*

**MILIBAND:** *(looks at autocue one last time, then decides, takes breath)* Actually - look. The autocue says I should close with something from the party that delivers. And I know - I know that is, right now, a very awkward sentence to end on. But I do want to say one thing genuinely, which is - *(audience quiets)* - the grid really is a physics problem, and the physics really does matter, and if anyone is watching this who works in NESO and has a very thick technical document they've been waiting for someone to read - send it to Ian. He'll print it.

**HISLOP:** *(surprised)* ...Fair enough.

**MERTON:** *(surprised)* I'll say this for him. He's no Boris.

**HISLOP:** Nobody's Boris.

**MILIBAND:** *(wistfully)* Nobody's Boris.

*Theme music.*

---

*POST-CREDITS OUTTAKE:*

*Floor manager approaches Miliband.*

**FLOOR MANAGER:** We need you to do the closing line again. With the coconuts.

**MILIBAND:** What coconuts?

**FLOOR MANAGER:** Boris brought coconuts once. When he was host. It became a thing.

**MILIBAND:** I don't have coconuts.

**FLOOR MANAGER:** *(looking around desperately, into radio)* Can someone get coconuts?

**MILIBAND:** Nobody is getting coconuts.

**MERTON:** *(from across studio)* Boris got coconuts.

**MILIBAND:** Boris was Prime Minister!

**HISLOP:** *(from across studio)* So was a tub of lard, briefly.

*Pause.*

**MILIBAND:** ...Can someone *actually* get coconuts?

*A runner sprints off. The audience applauds.*
